Synspire was an experimental project where Anton explored how AI, knowledge graphs and audience data could help musicians and creative teams make clearer decisions. How did Synspire work?

Synspire gathered public signals about an artist — music, influences, audience behaviour, online presence and performance — and connected them in a lightweight knowledge‑graph. Instead of treating data as separate points, it linked entities into a network that exposed relationships, patterns and growth pathways.

  • Identity & brand mapping: analysed themes, tones, similar artists and community signals to generate a structured “artist persona”.
  • Audience insight: surfaced engaged segments, adjacent communities and emerging growth clusters.
  • Venue & geography recommendations: mapped likely next venues, cities and online spaces using patterns from similar artists and local scenes.

The engine delivered insights in minutes rather than days. It didn’t try to predict fame or replace judgement — it reduced noise so decisions were clearer.
