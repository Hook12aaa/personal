What was Synspire?

Synspire was an experimental project where Anton explored how AI, knowledge graphs and audience data could help musicians and creative teams make clearer decisions. It began as a prototype that generated identity and audience insights for artists, and grew into a tool that could map local venues, online communities and growth pathways in minutes instead of days.

The goal wasn’t to build a full company at that stage, but to test whether AI could simplify an industry where data is fragmented and hard to interpret. Synspire produced early traction and taught Anton how to build a small product end‑to‑end — from prototyping and algorithms to user experience and narrative. The project paused when he shifted focus toward employment, but the ideas continue to shape his thinking about systems, identity and AI‑driven insight.

How did Synspire work?

Synspire gathered public signals about an artist — music, influences, audience behaviour, online presence and performance — and connected them in a lightweight knowledge‑graph. Instead of treating data as separate points, it linked entities into a network that exposed relationships, patterns and growth pathways.

  • Identity & brand mapping: analysed themes, tones, similar artists and community signals to generate a structured “artist persona”.
  • Audience insight: surfaced engaged segments, adjacent communities and emerging growth clusters.
  • Venue & geography recommendations: mapped likely next venues, cities and online spaces using patterns from similar artists and local scenes.

The engine delivered insights in minutes rather than days. It didn’t try to predict fame or replace judgement — it reduced noise so decisions were clearer.
